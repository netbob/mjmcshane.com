Hanging At Oak Creek - Part One

The jailhouse was on the edge of town looking down on the creek. Oak Creek was a small town with a small sized creek near an oak tree. Prisoners were asked *hanging or firing squad* when they were sentenced. Most picked hanging. Beyond the edge of the creek was Boot Hill. Hangings weren’t frequent so the tree served instead of a formal gallows. You died looking across a gentle creek or against the stark jail brick walls.

In the spring of 1870, the town was home to soldiers returning from the war between the states. At the south edge of the town was the mill. Towards the north end was the bar.

“Whisky barkeep!”

“Hold your horses Pete, todays the hangin at the tree so I'm swamped.”

“I just got back into town, who's the miscreant?”

“Sheriff said it's ol Sam Roberts.”

“What! I served with Sam, what's he done?”

“No one knows.”

The sound of a whiskey glass slammed down on the bar as Pete rushed out.

“Sheriff, open up, it’s Pete! I need to see Sam!”

“Hey Pete, come on in, I could use your help.”

“They told me down at the bar you got Sam for hangin today. What's he done?”

“That's the problem. Sam insisted on turning himself in. Says an accounting must be done.”

“Let me talk to him.”

Jail cells in Oak Creek were simple with a bare wooden cot. Sam sat on the edge of the cot.
